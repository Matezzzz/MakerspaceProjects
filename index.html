**Makerspace projects** # Lasercut maps *(December 2023)* I decided to make a map of Zurich to hang in my apartment and another one to gift my family for christmas. My design idea was as follows - I would have three layers, the bottom one was greypaper with lake and river waves printed on top, the middle one would be plywood with lakes cut out & shown parks, the top one would be all the roads, painted black. I created all my designs using the [Snazzy Maps](https://snazzymaps.com) website (each time only showing roads/parks/...), then I used GIMP to create the finished graphics, and finally inkscape for converting to vector graphics, which could be then printed on the lasercutters provided by the makerspace. The size of both maps was set to fit on the supported material while keeping the golden ration between height & width, $690$ x $426$ mm. # Ring of lights *(October 2023 -> TBA)* I have a ring of lights on the ceiling in my room! These were used by the previous owner of the now turned student house as backlight for psychological sessions held in what is my apartment today. However, the lights were disabled by the previous owner - so with the permission from the current landlord, I decided to try to fix them.
By connecting each light to the power grid individually, I figured the problem is in the controlling network, not the lights themselves, so I decided to fix and improve that. My goal was to be able to dim each light in real time by sending commands either from my pc or any other device. After getting some help from Lukas Pfitscher from the ETH Makerspace about which circuits I would want to use to achieve my goals, I came up with the following design - one arduino will control 5 lights using PWM, it will be able to receive commands via bluetooth. I will use a step down converter to obtain power for the arduino, and RobotDyn AC dimmers will be used to modulate the AC current using the PWM sent by the arduino. Then I will have one control network in the wall, that will be able to signal all the arduinos on the ceiling. I started by creating a demo circuit that is able to control one light by receiving bluetooth commands from my phone:

# Face chocolate *(October 2023)* I decided to create a chocolate in the shape of a friends face as a gift for his birthday. I started by taking his photo but converting it directly didn't look so promising, so I looked into edge detection algorithms and ways to convert images to pencil drawings. In the end, I settled on using photoshop to convert the photo to smoothed edges, and used a python script to interpret is as a heightmap. This gave me the basic shape of the 3D model. The first idea I had was to take an inverse of the face, add a joke nickname from a summer camp where we used to go together and to 3D print the whole thing. The result looked quite neat (though with a few 3D print hairs), but sadly, the chocolate was rather subpar. It had a tendency to stick to the form, and to take tiny pieces of plastic with it, making it inedible. Also, the text was mirrored:
Because of this, I looked into alternative ways to construct the mold, and quickly enough, I found a machine that would be able to do just that - the vacuum former at the makerspace can place a plastic sheet over my 3D print, and suck out all the air, forming a food-grade mould. So this is what I went with - after few struggling attempts with the 3D printers (the nozzle kept getting stuck because I was printing at the lowest resolution), I managed to get a succesful print, and created a mold using the vacuum former. The resulting treat looked and tasted great, particularly when cast out of the finest belgian chocolate :D
